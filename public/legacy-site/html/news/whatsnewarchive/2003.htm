19th October 2003
Revenue Officers Go Back to Nature

On Wednesday 15th October, 10 Inland Revenue Officers led by Mike Boyes from the Leeds Office took advantage of a scheme to aid local voluntary organisations. They had decided to give their time to help the Friends of Gledhow Valley Woods with a special Action Day, which included path maintenance and woodland management including stream clearing and tree pruning.

At the end of the day, seventeen metres of path including steps had been re-laid, part of the stream which had been blocked up had been cleared and some of the trees on the triangle of land at the junction of Gledhow Valley Road and Allerton Grange Way had been pruned back to improve the sight line for motorists and assist the grass cutters.

To see some pictures from the day and find out more about what went on, please see the Team Building Action Day For Leeds Tax Office Staff page in the Events Gallery section.

Foxes in the Smog

23rd September 2003
Gledhow Hall History Plus Other Site Updates

The whole About the Woods area of the site has been given a serious overhall, with the addition of a lot of new information and pictures. Brand new sections include...

  • World War One Voluntary Aid Detachment (VAD) Hospital
    A wonderful set of historical photographs showing Gledhow Hall during its use as a World War One VAD Hospital.
  • The Burmantofts Bathroom
    Details of the impressive hand-painted Burmantofts "Faience" tile bathroom, originally designed for the Prince of Wales' visit around 1885, including a 360 degree panorama.

Other updates to the site include the addition of A recent Yorkshire Evening Post article to the Press Clippings & Media Publicity section. Entitled "Adrian's Quick Thinking Leads to a Showdown", the article described the successful campaigning for traffic-calming measures along Gledhow Valley Road.

Also, minutes of the recent Core Group meeting have been added to the Minutes of Core Group Meetings & AGMs section, and the Guest Book Entries page has grown so much that it has now been split into two parts.

19th May 2003
Family Funday 2003 Pictures Now Online

This year's Family Funday was bigger and better than ever, and was very well attended despite the awful weather. A big thanks is due to all the FGVW Core Group and other volunteers who helped organise and run the event; to everyone who helped by donating home-made refreshments; to Tesco for providing extremely nice doughnuts, cookies and drinks; to the several hundred people who braved the rain to attend; and especially to FGVW Chair Martin Calvert who brought it all together.

Photos from the day are now available in the Events Gallery, and there's also a 360 panoramic image showing the event in full swing.

5th May 2003
Family Funday Coming Soon!

Friends of Gledhow Valley Woods invite you to their 4th Annual Family Funday

Date & Time:

  • Saturday May 17th 2003 (1.30pm - 4.30pm)

Location:

  • Grassland area adjacent to Gledhow Valley Road and Allerton Grange Way.

Come along and enjoy all the activities and find out just what the Friends of Gledhow Valley Woods are doing to enhance your local woodlands.

Activities include:

  • Marquees with displays, live music, home-made refreshments, and fun & games.
  • Circus skills workshop, face painting, and "Daftasadrum" drum workshop.
  • Build a bird box, create your own "meadow in a pot", decorate a t-shirt, make wind chimes, dragonflies and wishing sticks.
  • See some "mini beasts", bats, bees, and other wildlife guests.
  • Demonstrations from "Treesponsibility" and Knott Wood Coppicers.
  • See our plans for the woods and calming the traffic on Gledhow Valley Road.
  • Much more...!
Have a look in the Events Gallery to find out what happened at last year's Family Funday.

15th April 2003
Second Radio Leeds Breakfast Show FGVW Interview Online!

The second interview between Radio Leeds and the Friends of Gledhow Valley Woods regarding the blocked sewer in the woods is now available to listen to online, or to read in transcript form, in the Press Clippings & Media Publicity section.

The interview concerned the successful outcome to the problem of raw effluence leaking straight down the side of Gledhow Valley from a blocked sewer.

It was first broadcast on the Radio Leeds Breakfast Show between 6.55am and 7.00am on Thursday 6th March 2003 March 2003.

8th March 2003
Radio Leeds Breakfast Show FGVW Interview Online!

If you missed the recent interview between Radio Leeds and the Friends of Gledhow Valley Woods regarding the blocked sewer in the woods don't worry... it is now available to listen to online, or to read in transcript form, in the Press Clippings & Media Publicity section.

The interview concerned the problem of raw effluence leaking straight down the side of Gledhow Valley from a blocked sewer, and the long running battle with Yorkshire Water, Leeds City Council and the Environment Agency to try and get something done about it.

It was first broadcast on the Radio Leeds Breakfast Show between 7.06am and 7.13am on Wednesday 5th March 2003.
