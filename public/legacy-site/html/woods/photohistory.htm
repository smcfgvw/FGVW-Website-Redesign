1925: Constructing Gledhow Valley Road
 

16th October 1925. Five small wooden rods stick out of the earth where the road is being built, and two mounds of earth lie to the right. Makeshift tracks head off to the north in two directions. Raised woodland can be seen to the left, and dense woodland to the right.

16th October 1925. Early construction of Gledhow Valley Road. Two men with spades stand in a large area of rubble and earth. Five men stand in the centre of the picture with a railway track to the right. A trench has been made to the far right, and a fence blocks access to a field.

The Railway Foundry's first petrol locomotive, a 25 horse power 2ft gauge locomotive, shown working on the construction of Gledhow Valley Road.

   

1934: Gipton Beck Covering
 

27th August 1934. Gledhow Valley showing the beck covering for Gipton Beck. Men with a horse and cart stand in the background.

   

1948: Constructing the Weir for Gledhow Valley Lake
 

19th August 1948. Close up view of weir under construction as part of Gledhow Valley Lake.

   

1956: Constructing Gledhow Valley Lake / Gledhow Beck
 

6th June 1956. Excavations for Gledhow Valley Lake. A row of council houses can be seen on the skyline.

6th June 1956. Gledhow Valley Lake under construction.

6th June 1956. Gledhow Valley Lake under construction. A workman stands behind a dragline being used to widen the watercourse.

Gledhow Beck in 1956.
