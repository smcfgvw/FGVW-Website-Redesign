6. Work on Audrey's Meadow and Ponds

The contractor is hoping to start work in the Autumn. One of the large ponds (number 3) has been damaged and is nearly empty. One of the smaller ponds (number 2) is only just filling up. These will be looked at when the contractor starts work. There is also a large pile of brush and vegetation that could potentially be a fire hazard and is in the wrong place. A suggestion as made to move the brush and spread it out thinner to make wildlife habitats. The contractor could help by using a digger.

8. AOB

  • Benches
    Ashling Donnelly has requested a bench to be placed looking down towards the lake at the swans. The Group will look into it as it is getting quite crowded with benches in that area. The Group discussed alternatives for how people could donate to the Group and have memorials. Also the Group do not make any money on the benches and donations just go to pay for the bench and materials.
  • Banking Update
    Paul notified the Group that there is currently £9,784.86p in the account as we have received money from the Foundation.
  • Daffodils
    Joan has been investigated getting some free daffodils. We are too late this year but we can apply next year. Paul suggested planting wild daffodils but the Group has had limited success with these in the past. It was mentioned that some of the daffodils are getting picked.
  • Rubbish Dumping
    Steven raised the subject of rubbish being dumped in the woods near the top of Little Switzerland. It is difficult to get to due to the slope and the bluebells but if there are enough volunteers at the Action Morning the Group agreed to make a start on clearing the area. There are also a couple of campsite areas are in the woods which need to be removed by the Council.
  • Allerton Grange Fields
    Joan informed the Group that Allerton Grange Fields have been adopted by Parks and Countryside and will remain a green area.
