6. Groundwork Application

All work is due to be completed by the end of February with the exception of the ironwork on gateway one which will be completed by the end of March. An opening ceremony has been suggested.

Martin will be contacting the local school to arrange a mid-week opening day where the children can scatter wildflower seeds and have refreshments. This is planned for mid-April.

Martin also suggested that we apply for a sustainability grant to help sustain the project, although we have to apply for this by the end of February.

7. Grants

The Group are currently spending around £4,000 per year. The main sources of income are the FunDay and general merchandise sales. These raise around £2,000 per year. For the last few years we have been using a further £2,000 from our reserves. The Group currently have £6,000 left in reserve so the Group need to consider obtaining grants for the work that they do.

Adrian has done some investigating and has found several grants available but in order to apply the Group need to submit specific projects for the money. Also some grants expect some form of price matching from the Group. The Group proposed various projects such as path work and building and repairing steps, which could include quotes for stone, wood and wildflowers.

Adrian also agreed to contact local Councillors such as Jane Dawson, to see if there was any Council money available. Paul to provide a copy of the Constitution, accounts and bank statements to support any grant applications.

9. AOB

  • Gledhow Valey Conservation Area Group
    Christine Osborne attended the meeting and updated the Group on their recent event held at Gledhow Sports and Social Club. She thanked the Group for their raffle prize donation. Christine has been investigating grants for the GVCAG and has obtained £500 for a display board. She was surprised to find that display boards were also going up in the woods as part of the Brackenwoods Project. She suggested that it might be beneficial for the groups to work together in future to obtain grants.
  • Black Bags
    Martin agreed to obtain some black bags.
  • Retired Woodcutter
    Andrew is a retired woodcutter and Steve Clavering has agreed that he can help to identify and remove tress in the wood. Joan agreed to get in contact with him.
  • Yorkshire Gardens Trust
    The Group and the Council are in contact with Louise. It has been agreed that Steve Clavering we go round the woods with members of the Trust. A volunteer from the Group will also take them round the Bath House and show them the work that they have done in the woods. A request has been made for this to be on Sunday prior to the Bath House Open Heritage Day, with refreshments and a gazebo. The Group had originally planned to clean the Bath House on this day so this will be moved to the Saturday. A volunteer from Gledhow Valley Conservation Area Group has also been requested to talk to the Trust about the buildings in the area.
