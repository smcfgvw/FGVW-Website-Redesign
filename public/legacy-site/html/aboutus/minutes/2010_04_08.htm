7. Friends of Allerton Grange

Charlotte Parkin and Bhupinder Dev attended the meeting and gave an interesting talk on the work their Group is doing in the Allerton Grange area particularly around the new and old school locations. The Group was started last year when work began on the new Allerton Grange School as residents were concerned over potential increases in litter, traffic issues and anti-social behaviour.

The Friends have been working with local residents, police, and schools. The green space where the original school was demolished was handed back to the Council in June. The Group are consulting local residents to gather opinions on what the land should be used for. It currently contains several football pitches but there is a call for the land to be a multi-purpose recreational area. There is also a call for a toddler (under 5s) area as the one near Talbot Primary School has been lost.

The Friends explained that the area had historical links with Kirkstall Abbey and that the fields help form a natural green corridor connection to Gledhow Valley Woods. There is an opportunity to do more planting along the side of the school and an opportunity for biodiversity.

The area affects both Roundhay and Moortown Wards. The Friends:

  • Asked FGVW to raise awareness of their campaign;
  • Asked to link up with our website;
  • Welcomed members of the Group to attend their meetings;
  • Asked for Group members to provide input and consultation on ideas.

The Group invited the Friends of Allerton Grange to their FunDay so that they could get ideas from local residents on what to do with the Green Space.
