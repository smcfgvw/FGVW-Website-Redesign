7. Sewage

Joan has been on a site visit with Yorkshire Water as sewage is going into the beck. Yorkshire Water has stated that they are not responsible as tree roots have damaged a drainage system not under their control, but that they will help anyway.

Wendy from North East Homes has been in contact with Joan as they are responsible for surface land drainage. They admitted looking down the wrong hole but now they have the right one and their surveyors are looking into it! Once the problem has been fixed Yorkshire Water has agreed to clean up the area so the problem can be monitored more easily.

9. AOB

  • Gledhow Valley Conservation Area Group
    The Gledhow Valley Conservation Group would like to organise a walk in the woods on 30th April in celebration of the Royal Wedding and connections between the Middleton Family and the woods. However, they need Public Liability Insurance to cover this. They have approached the Group asking if they can somehow use our insurance. The Group discussed the pros and cons of doing this including possible higher premiums resulting from a claim, additional costs of adding the group, and additional risk assessments. Also our constitution structure doesn't allow for affiliate groups. Paul agreed to contact the insurance company and the Group agreed that if it was going to be complicated and costly that this would not happen.
  • Daffodils
    The Group were excited to see the daffodils growing which they planted 5 years ago. Unfortunately many of the flowers were picked for Mother's Day!
  • Rubbish Clearance
    Joan raised the issue of rubbish and furniture in the woods around the site where the tent is near the big house. There is also a mattress in the woods to clear. Some trees also look like they are about to fall over on to the paths. Joan agreed to arrange a site visit with Elaine to see what could be done to fix the problems.
  • Duckling Barrier
    Simon Cunliffe has been contacted to see if the Duckling Barrier, mentioned last year, could be installed by Yorkshire Water to try and prevent a recurrence of last year's duckling losses.
