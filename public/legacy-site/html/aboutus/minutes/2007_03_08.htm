2. Minutes of the Meeting Held 8th February 2007

Agreed as a true record.

3. Matters Arising

  • Duffy's
    Duffey's have finished working in the woods. They have been working on the silt wells and now the water is flowing correctly. They have also removed some blockages in the stream at the Allerton Grange Way end of the woods. The Group are very pleased with the work they have done.

4. Correspondence

The Group received the following correspondence:

  • The Group has received an e-mail concerning the proposal to increase the height of a mobile phone mast on the football ground near Bracken Edge. Martin agreed to e-mail Adam with the details so he could distribute to the Group members with information of how to object to the decision. The closing date for the appeal is 14th March.
  • Details have been received from the Wildflower company from whom the Group ordered flowers last year. Steve agreed to contact Elaine about a list of suitable bog plants.
  • The Group has received this month's minutes of the Leeds Voice environmental forum.
  • Adrian provided details on the appeal decision for flats at Gledhow House. The appeal was rejected, however this was only by a very small margin. The appeal was rejected over car parking and because the area was now a designated Environmental Area.
  • The Group has been invited to a meeting given by the Conservation Area Group. Chris Osborne has decided to continue with the Group following the work they did in achieving conservation area status last year. The first meeting was held on Wednesday afternoon and the Group did not have a representative free that day. The next meeting is on Monday 26th March at 7.30pm so Martin will attend or Mike will go in his place. Chris has asked if they can have a stall at our FunDay, which the Group had no objections to.

5. Grants Sub-Committee

Elaine has sent the Group a grant application from the Recreational Non-Sporting Organisation which Martin sent on to Peter Traynor. Martin agreed to liase with Elaine and get back to Peter on what the grant was for.

Adrian agreed to apply for some MICE money for the FunDay. Alex suggested trying Paul Wadsworth and said that he would ask the Chapel Allerton Committee.

Adrian met with 3 councillors on Monday to discuss our grant application from the Area Committee for building the path around the lake. They discussed what the Group wanted to do. Adrian showed photos of the areas where work needed to be done to improve access and also showed them examples of the work the Group had done already. Adrian is waiting to hear if we have been successful.

Adrian and Peter agreed to visit RTC (Resource in the Community) on Roundhay Road to use their FunderFinder system.

Paul has sent in an application to the Wade's Charity for general administrative expenses.

6. Action Mornings

The next Action Morning is on Sunday 11th March. The Rhododendrums need cutting back. The Group raised some Health & Safety concerns over burning the cuttings and agreed to tape an area off. Joan suggested bringing barrows and shovels to get rid of the mud on the path and use some of the chippings on the steps. Steve agreed to brings some spades, barrows and rakes. Martin also raised the problem of Knotweed to Steve who agreed to investigate.

The following Action Morning is on Sunday 15th April. Meeting place to be where Allerton Grange Way meets Gledhow Valley Road. The Group would like to get started on the path work but need to wait until the 106 money has been agreed. Elaine has got 2 gabions available so the Group could put these in during the Action Morning if the grant has still not been agreed.

Martin agreed to provide a list of Action Mornings for the remainder of the year.

7. 106 Grant Update

Martin has a meeting with Fred Duff on Tuesday 13th March. They intend to walk round the woods and discuss the proposed work the Group which to carry out with the 106 money.

Martin has received a quote of £1,775 + VAT from Airedale Tree Services to clear the island in the Gledhow Lake, which the Group thought was a reasonable price.

The Group agreed at the last meeting that the quotes from BTCV were very high so Martin had got a quote from Rightway. Their quote for the same amount of path work was approximately £4,600.

Steve thought that he may be able to get some 'free' days from BTCV and agreed to investigate.
