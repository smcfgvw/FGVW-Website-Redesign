3. Matters Arising

  • David Miles wishes to purchase a couple of water quality monitoring items funded by FGVW. It was recommended he liaise with Steve Jones-Blackett.
  • The Economic and Social Research Council (ERSC) has awarded £1million funding to Leeds and York universities for Citizen Science water quality monitoring projects. This will allow for monitoring of heavy metals and pharmaceuticals.
  • Leeds City Council (LCC) has paid for the River Stewardship Company (RSC) to clear debris at the dam end of the lake to enable water quality monitoring to take place.
  • David Miles is chasing LCC for action regarding the 5 properties with cross-connections to the infamous surface water drain. Yorkshire Water has sent letters to the 5 properties and LCC needs to ensure resolution.
  • There is now 12 months of data on water quality to make a report to send to councillors, politicians and public bodies.

6. Project to Eradicate Bamboo and Knotweed and Create Wetland off Gledhow Lane

There was an online meeting on 19th September 2024. Costs of removal of have increased so as much soil as possible will be left on site in the embankment. The soil will be chemically sprayed instead of being removed. As a result, wording of notices will include the wording "Dog owners are advised to keep dogs on leads whilst the work is taking place". Areas of work will be fenced off and "Spraying taking place" notices will be erected.

Puddle clay, rather than liner, will be used to create the wet areas. Trees in the affected areas will be protected.

7. Chapel Allerton Festival / Bath House Opening

Recently discovered boxes of FGVW merchandise were heavily discounted and sold at the Chapel Allerton Arts Festival.

Takings from Festival £506.56, compared to £504.10 in 2023. There was lots of footfall and it was a profitable day.

Takings from Bath House £123.41, compared to £270.74 in 2023. The weather and the Green Fair opening of the Bath House were part reasons for the lower takings.
