3. Matters Arising

  • Leeds City Council
    Adrian confirmed that the Leeds City Council Small Grant application has been agreed and the Group has received £500. £350 of this is to be used for specific web site development projects.

  • Traffic Calming
    Work is ongoing.

  • Tree Cutting
    The Trees were due to be cut back to improve the light on Gledhow Valley Road but this has not been done. The Group has been in contact with Justin to see when this will be done.

  • Drainage Covers
    John Casey has put some large boulders over the drainage covers to try and prevent the area from being drained. The Group feel that this area does not need draining.

  • Ponds Trust
    A representative from the Ponds Trust was unable to attend tonight's meeting due to other commitments.

4. Action Mornings

The last Action Morning on 9th February had a poor turn out of just 14 people. Bark chippings were put on the path and steps near the lake and Gledhow Lane.

The next Action Morning will be on Sunday 13th March The faulty brush cutter has been replaced and it is hoped that this can be used.

After that, the next Action Morning will be Sunday 17th April. The Group is hoping to cut back the rhodedendrums near the concrete footbridge. Please meet at the small footbridge near the junction of Gledhow Valley Road and Gledhow Park Road.

6. AOB

  • NEAT Scheme
    Martin has received details on this scheme which is a program to support community groups at no cost. The scheme will provide free training with a trial session at Adrian Coltman's house on 2nd April from 2pm to 4pm. Members are welcome to attend (please contact us if you would like more details).

  • Gledhow Beck
    The bank along the stream heading towards Bracken Woods is being undercut at the point were the stream narrows. Martin Calvert is to ask Joe Smallman for some guidance on how to tackle this problem. This work may involve widening the stream or adding rock baskets. Any work necessary will be completed in the summer when the water levels are low.

  • Ponds Trust
    Peter is going to contact Hugh from the Ponds Trust to arrange a special meeting at his convenience as it has been difficult for him to attend normal meetings due to other commitments.

  • Hazardous Hole
    Joan raised the awareness of a hole near the small bridge which has appeared since the flooding. Water is oozing out of the hole and is about 4ft deep. The Group will investigate to see what it is and if it is dangerous before deciding what action to take.

  • Grants
    Adrian is going to look into other grants which may be available. He will also contact B&Q who donate unsaleable goods to local charities and voluntary groups.

  • Community Forums
    There is a Community Chapel Allerton Village Forum meeting on Thursday 17th March at 6.30pm at St Matthews. Everyone welcome.

  • PCSO's
    The local PCSO's have issued warnings to some of the kids that have been using motorbikes in the woods.
