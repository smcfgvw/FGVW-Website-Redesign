4. Correspondence

Christine Osborne (of Gledhow Valley Conservation Area Group) updated the Group on developments with the illegal four storey house built in place of the proposed garage at 11 Old Park Road. The building work is in breach of the agreed planning permission. The Roundhay Planning Forum would like to invite all local community groups to pool together to write and add weight to the continued opposition.

The Planning Panel is looking to negotiate a compromise, however the building has become unlawful. The local groups are concerned that planning regulations and conservation area guidance reports are worthless if they are not adhered to. This could lead to others flouting planning regulations within the conservation area. They are looking for as much support as possible from local groups are as individuals.

The Group agreed to support the case but left the decision to Martin as to whether we support as a Group or as individuals.

6. Groundwork Application

The application has passed through to the next stage following the site visit. They are looking to see if one of the paths could be adapted for wheelchair use, but it could be too steep. The proposal is going through to the next planning stage.

7. Roundhay Live

A large REAP event took place on Saturday 18 June 2011. It was a chance for all local community groups to get together to promote the "sustainable community". Joan and Mervyn attended on behalf of the Group. There were a few teething problems as there was no tent allocated for the Group (despite the fact that the tents belonged to FGVW!) and no communication regarding timings of allowing vehicles on the field. However, it was a successful day and the Group are happy to support it again next year. It was suggested that there be a 2-3 week gap between our FunDay and this event.

The Group loaned marquees and pop-up gazebos for the event, and agreed that they were happy to do so again. One gazebo was damaged on the day but the Group were compensated.
