6. AOB

  • Pastures
    The Group was asked by some local residents to clear the pastures of rubbish and overgrown bushes. This was done some time ago however since then some complaints have been received from residents claiming this could have contributed to an increase in burglaries in the area. Although there is no evidence to support this the Group decided not to do any future work in this area.

  • Kerb Stones
    The Group asked the Council to remove the Kerb Stones left in the grassland area during the traffic calming works as bulbs had been planted there last year. The Council removed the stones using a large digger - scooping up the ground in the process, so it is unlikely that any bulbs have survived.

  • Kingfishers
    A pair of kingfishers have been spotted on several occasions near the lake.

  • Lake Overflowing
    The lake is currently overflowing. Ground Drainage has been to clear the drains in the area as they think there may be a blockage. The lake usually only overflows after heavy rainfall over a prolonged period.

  • Action Morning
    Donna is now authorised to advise the Group on what to do if children attend the Action Mornings. We need to add a disclaimer on Duty of Care so that it is clear that adults who bring children are responsible for them.

  • Bath House
    Lynda Kitching, Vice Chairman of the Leeds Civic Trust has written to the Group to state that the Bath House could be added to the Heritage Open Days this year. The Group would need to clean up the Bath House and may also need to arrange a walk in the woods. The Heritage Open Days will run from Thursday 8th September to Sunday 11th September inclusive. Volunteers are welcome to help support this venture.

  • Hepatitis
    A local GP has expressed concern over the lack of precautions being taken by the Group regarding the risk of infection whilst working in the woods. She has advised members to speak to their GP about Tetanus and Hepatitis B injections.

  • Rats
    The amount of rats has increased in the woods again. The `ratman' is to be contacted.

  • Yorkshire Gardens Trust
    Martin Lynch has been in contact with Paul. He thinks that some parts of the Trust may be relevant to the Group and some grants may be applicable to the work we do.

  • FGVW Web Site
    Adam asked the Group is he could have £30 to license some utility software for use on the web site, and this was agreed.
