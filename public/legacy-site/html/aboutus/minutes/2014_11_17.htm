6. FGVW Christmas Cards

The Group has received a new batch of Christmas cards and Diane and Joan have been very busy selling them to the public. Wendy has kindly given a generous discount to the Group as a donation which the Group is very grateful for. The "With Love" shop on Roundhay Road has taken 24 cards to try out.

Martin and Adrian were also keen to do a pop-up stall in Chapel Allerton.

8. Woodland Management Program

Martin has received an e-mail from Justin Williamson (Leeds City Council Forestry Officer) concerning a few complaints from the public. Justin had offered to meet people to discuss the plans but no one had taken him up on this offer. He has also offered to do a walk in the woods and talk to the public about the plans. FGVW has e-mailed the Councillors to discuss the plans also. Discussions are continuing, however the Group was keen to stress that the woodland is on Council property and any work will be carried out by Leeds City Council and not FGVW.

This is a long term management plan for the woods to improve it for the future. The Group feel it is irresponsible not to do anything and work like this has happened in the past without detriment. The changes should lead to sustainability, improvement in wildlife and diversity.

9. Audrey's Meadow

Kevin from Potternewton Park assisted the Group using his tractor to help clear some tree stumps and logs in the meadow. He will also bring a special tool to help remove the roots. One of the ponds has been filled in with soil from the meadow. The Group were going to leave the ground free for a year and then plant wildflowers in March 2016. However, it was suggested that we plant some annuals for next season. The Group will get advice from Kay who consults on wildflowers and meadows. The Group also agreed to look into grants for wildflower seed.

10. AOB

  • Ash/Poplar Trees
    Geoff raised the problem of the Ash and Poplar trees getting too large and needing to be thinned. Also some of the Poplars look like they have been marked for felling. Martin agreed to contact the Council to find out what was happening as originally these trees were supposed to be thinned every ten years.
  • The Wall
    Trevor asked when the Group would be able to fix the wall. It was agreed that this should be done in the spring after the frost. Mervyn suggested using the stone to drop the wall down one level. It was also suggested that the path and steps needed repairing in that area and could be done at the same time.

The Chair would like it noted that Joan did not have any other business this month!
